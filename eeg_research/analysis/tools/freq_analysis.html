eeg_research.analysis.tools.freq_analysis

This module handle frequency spectrum obtained from an FFT.

The different calculations perfomed on the spectrum follow a methodology used in Fast Periodic Visual Stimulation (FPVS) to quantify with a high Signal Noise Ratio some cognitive processes at a specific frequency (e.g. the frequency of an Oddball). It is inspired from the Rossion et al. 2014 and Jonas et al. 2016.

Note:

The articles mentionned perform a Steady State Evoked Related Potential (SSVEP) paradigm which is called Fast Periodic Visual Stimulation (Rossion et al. 2014). The paradigm involves a visual stimulation (images) at a base frequency (6 Hz). Every 5 images, an oddball is pressented (A face among non face images, or a known face among uknown faces etc.). The odball frequency is 6/5 = 1.2 Hz. In order to quantify the electrophysiological response of the cognitive process exhibited by the oddball, the authors perform a frequency tagging analysis of the signal. They calculate the fast fourier transform (FFT) of the signal and then perform different operations and claculations on the spectrum. The different operations are the correction of the baseline, the zscore (to quantify the significance of the amplitude of the frequency of interest from the "noise"), and the signal to noise ratio. To do so, they consider a specific zone of 50 surrounding bins around the frequency of interest (25 bins before and 25 bins after the frequency of interest). They leave out one bin before and one bin after the frequency of interest that lead to 24 bins each side of the frequency of interest. This zone is consider as the "surrounding noise" of the brain activity and the amplitude of the frequency of interest is considered as the "signal". Therefore, the zscore is calculated as the amplitude of the signal divided by the standard deviation of the surrounding noise. The signal to noise ratio is calculated as the amplitude of the signal divided by the mean amplitude of the surrounding noise. Their methdology was implemented for a signal sampled at 512 Hz. Therefore 1 bin = 0.0135 Hz. In this module the approach used is in frequency step rather than frequency bin, in order to have the same frequency values as in the articles still offering flexibility in term of input sampling frequency and frequency resolution.

This whole approach is interesting to quantify also noise that are at a known frequency (e.g. 60 Hz noise from the electrical grid, gradient artifacts for EEG-fMRI).

 83class Spectrum:
 84    """A class to store and manipulate the Fourier Spectrum of a signal."""
 85
 86    def __init__(self) -> None:
 87        """Initialize."""
 88        self.info: dict = dict()
 89        self.info["process_history"] = list()
 90
 91    def calculate_fft(self, raw: mne.io.Raw) -> "Spectrum":
 92        """Initialize the Spectrum object.
 93
 94        Args:
 95            raw (mne.io.Raw): The raw signal to be analyzed
 96        """
 97        self.sampling_rate = raw.info["sfreq"]
 98        self.signal = raw.get_data()
 99        self._adjust_signal_length()
100        self.signal_length = np.shape(self.signal)[1]
101        self.spectrum = scipy.fft.rfft(self.signal) * 2 / self.signal_length
102        self.frequencies = scipy.fft.fftfreq(
103            self.spectrum.shape[1], 1 / self.sampling_rate
104        )
105
106        self.frequency_resolution = self.frequencies[1] - self.frequencies[0]
107        self.info["units"] = "V"
108        self.info["sfreq"] = self.sampling_rate
109        self.info["frequency_resolution"] = self.frequency_resolution
110        self.info["n_channels"] = self.signal.shape[0]
111        self.info["ch_names"] = raw.info["ch_names"]
112
113        return self
114
115    def _adjust_signal_length(self) -> "Spectrum":
116        """Adjust the signal length to be a power of 2.
117
118        This is done in order to have a faster computation of the fft.
119
120        Args:
121        signal (numpy.ndarray): A 2D numpy array (channels, time).
122
123        Returns:
124        Self: the Spectrum object
125        """
126        current_length = self.signal.shape[1]
127
128        if current_length & (current_length - 1) != 0:
129            new_length = 2 ** int(np.log2(current_length))
130            self.signal = np.resize(self.signal, (self.signal.shape[0], new_length))
131
132        self.info["process_history"].append("Signal length adjusted to be a power of 2")
133
134        return self
135
136    def _set_frequency_of_interest(
137        self, frequency_of_interest: float = 12
138    ) -> "Spectrum":
139        """Set the frequency of interest.
140
141        This is following the methodology of Rossion et al. 2014 and Jonas et al. 2016
142        in order to get the amplitude of the surrounding bins.
143
144        Args:
145            frequency_of_interest (float): The frequency of interest.
146
147        Returns:
148            typing.Self: The Spectrum object
149        """
150        self.frequency_of_interest = frequency_of_interest
151        self.info["process_history"].append(
152            f"Frequency of interest set to {frequency_of_interest}"
153        )
154        return self
155
156    def _frequency_of_interest_exists(self) -> bool:
157        """Check if the frequency of interest has been set.
158
159        Returns:
160            bool: True if the frequency of interest has been set, False otherwise.
161        """
162        return hasattr(self, "frequency_of_interest")
163
164    def _get_frequency_index(self, frequency: np.ndarray | float) -> np.ndarray:
165        """Get the index of the frequency of interest in the spectrum.
166
167        Args:
168            frequency (np.ndarray | float): The frequency of interest.
169
170        Returns:
171            int: The index (position) in the spectrum
172        """
173        if isinstance(frequency, np.ndarray):
174            freq_per_electrode = np.broadcast_to(
175                self.frequencies, (frequency.shape[0], self.frequencies.shape[0])
176            )
177
178            frequencies_index = np.argmin(
179                np.abs(
180                    np.subtract(
181                        frequency.reshape(freq_per_electrode.shape[0], 1),
182                        freq_per_electrode,
183                    )
184                ),
185                axis=1,
186            )
187
188        elif isinstance(frequency, (int, float)):
189            frequencies_index = np.argmin(
190                np.abs(self.frequencies - frequency),
191            )
192
193        else:
194            raise TypeError("The frequency has to be a float or an array of floats")
195
196        return frequencies_index.astype(int)
197
198    def _get_amplitude_surounding_bins(
199        self,
200        desired_frequency_step: float = 0.0135,
201        nb_steps: int = 25,
202    ) -> np.ndarray:
203        """Get the amplitude of the surrounding bins of the frequency of interest.
204
205        Args:
206            frequency_of_interest (float ): The frequency around
207            desired_frequency_step (float, optional): _description_. Defaults to 0.0135
208                                                      to reproduce the articles
209                                                      methodology.
210            nb_steps (int, optional): _description_. Defaults to 25.
211
212        Returns:
213            np.ndarray: _description_
214        """
215        frequency_index = self._get_frequency_index(self.frequency_of_interest)
216
217        nb_bins = int(desired_frequency_step / self.frequency_resolution)
218        indices_left = np.linspace(
219            frequency_index - nb_steps * nb_bins,
220            frequency_index - nb_bins,
221            axis=1,
222        )
223
224        indices_right = np.linspace(
225            frequency_index + nb_bins,
226            frequency_index + nb_steps * nb_bins,
227            axis=1,
228        )
229
230        amplitude_surrounding_left_bins = self.spectrum[
231            np.arange(frequency_index.shape[0]), indices_left.astype(int).T
232        ]
233
234        amplitude_surrounding_right_bins = self.spectrum[
235            np.arange(frequency_index.shape[0]), indices_right.astype(int).T
236        ]
237
238        self.info["process_history"].append(
239            f"""Amplitude of the {(nb_steps-1)*2} surrounding bins of the
240            frequency of interest calculated with a frequency step of
241            {desired_frequency_step} Hz"""
242        )
243
244        return np.concatenate(
245            (amplitude_surrounding_left_bins.T, amplitude_surrounding_right_bins.T),
246            axis=1,
247        )
248
249    def _get_baseline(self) -> float:
250        """Get the baseline of the spectrum around the frequency of interest.
251
252        The baseline is the mean amplitude of the surrounding bins of the
253        frequency of interest.
254
255        Returns:
256            float : The baseline value
257
258        """
259        if not self._frequency_of_interest_exists():
260            raise ValueError("The frequency of interest has to be set first.")
261        amplitude_surrounding_bins = self._get_amplitude_surounding_bins()
262        return np.mean(amplitude_surrounding_bins, axis=1).reshape(-1, 1)
263
264    def correct_baseline(self) -> "Spectrum":
265        """Remove the baseline of the spectrum.
266
267        It removes the baseline on the entire spectrum. What is considered
268        as the baseline is the mean amplitude of the surrounding bins of the
269        frequency of interest (see _get_baseline() method for more details).
270        WARNING: This method is not reversible and will modify the spectrum.
271        Consider using the copy() method before using this method if you want
272        to keep the original spectrum.
273
274        Args:
275            frequency_of_interest (float, optional): The frequency around which
276                                                     the baseline, snr and
277                                                     zscore will be calculated.
278                                                     Defaults to 12.
279
280        Returns:
281            self : The modified Spectrum object.
282        """
283        baseline = self._get_baseline()
284        self.info["process_history"].append("Baseline corrected")
285        self.spectrum = np.subtract(self.spectrum, baseline)
286        return self
287
288    def _baseline_corrected(self) -> bool:
289        """Check if the baseline has been corrected.
290
291        Returns:
292            bool: True if the baseline has been corrected, False otherwise.
293        """
294        return "basline corrected" in self.info["process_history"]

296    def average_across_channel(self: "Spectrum") -> "Spectrum":
297        """Calculate the average across channels."""
298        self.spectrum = np.mean(self.spectrum, axis=0, keepdims=True)
299        self.spectrum_std = np.std(self.spectrum, axis=0, keepdims=True)
300
301        return self

303    def pick_channels(self, channel_names: list) -> "Spectrum":
304        """Pick the desired channel.
305
306        The spectrum contain only the desired channels
307
308        Args:
309            channel_names (list, optional): Defaults to None.
310
311        Returns:
312            Spectrum: The spectrum object
313        """
314        channel_truth_table = [
315            channel_name in channel_names for channel_name in self.info["ch_names"]
316        ]
317        self.spectrum = self.spectrum[channel_truth_table, :]
318        return self

320    def calculate_amplitude(self) -> "Spectrum":
321        """Calculate the amplitude of the spectrum.
322
323        Args:
324            spectrum (Spectrum): The Spectrum object
325                                        to be converted.
326
327        Returns:
328            Spectrum: The converted Spectrum object.
329        """
330        self.spectrum = np.abs(self.spectrum)
331        self.info["units"] = "V"
332        return self

334    def calculate_zscore(self) -> "Spectrum":
335        """Calculate the zscore of the spectrum.
336
337        The zscore is calculated as the amplitude of the frequency of interest
338        divided by the standard deviation of the amplitude of the surrounding
339        bins.
340
341        Args:
342            spectrum (Spectrum): The Spectrum object to be
343                                        converted.
344            frequency_of_interest (int, optional): The frequency around
345                                                   which the standard
346                                                   deviation is calculated.
347                                                   Defaults to 12.
348
349        Raises:
350            TypeError: The input has to be an AmplitudeSpectrum to calculate
351                       the zscore.
352
353        Returns:
354            Spectrum: The converted Spectrum object.
355        """
356        if not self._frequency_of_interest_exists():
357            raise ValueError("The frequency of interest has to be set first.")
358
359        if not self._baseline_corrected():
360            self.correct_baseline()
361
362        amplitude_surrounding_bins = self._get_amplitude_surounding_bins()
363
364        surrounding_bin_std = np.std(amplitude_surrounding_bins, axis=1).reshape(-1, 1)
365
366        self.spectrum = np.divide(self.spectrum, surrounding_bin_std)
367        self.info["process_history"].append("Zscore calculated")
368        self.info["units"] = "A.U"
369
370        return self
371
372    def calculate_snr(self) -> "Spectrum":
373        """Calculate the signal to noise ratio of the spectrum.
374
375        The signal to noise ratio is calculated as the baseline corrected
376        amplitude of the frequency of interest divided by the baseline
377        amplitude.
378
379        Args:
380            spectrum (Spectrum): The Spectrum object to be
381                                        converted.
382            frequency_of_interest (int, optional): The frequency around
383                                                   which the baseline and
384                                                   snr will be calculated.
385                                                   Defaults to 12.
386
387        Raises:
388            TypeError: The input has to be an AmplitudeSpectrum to calculate
389                       the snr.
390
391        Returns:
392            Spectrum: The converted Spectrum object.
393        """
394        if not self._frequency_of_interest_exists():
395            raise ValueError("The frequency of interest has to be set first.")
396
397        baseline = self._get_baseline()
398        self.spectrum = 20 * np.log10(np.divide(self.spectrum, baseline))
399        self.info["process_history"].append("Snr calculated")
400        self.info["units"] = "dB"
401        return self
402
403    def calculate_phase(self) -> "Spectrum":
404        """Calculate the phase of the spectrum.
405
406        Returns:
407            Self: The modified Spectrum object.
408        """
409        self.phase = np.angle(self.spectrum)
410        self.info["process_history"].append("Phase calculated")
411        self.info["units"] = "rad"
412        return self

414    def copy(self) -> "Spectrum":
415        """Copy the object following the same philosophy as mne objects.
416
417        Returns:
418            self: A copy of the instance

420        self.info["process_history"].append("Object copied")

424    def get_peak_magnitude(self, frequency_window: tuple = (17, 20)) -> "Spectrum":
425        """Get the peak magnitude and frequency within a specific window.
426
427        Args:
428            frequency_window (tuple, optional): The chosen frequency window
429                                                to get the max amplitude
430
431        Returns:
432            dict: A dictionary containing the window, the peak magnitude and
433                  the peak frequency.
434        """
435        if isinstance(frequency_window, tuple):
436            self.index_window = [
437                self._get_frequency_index(frequency) for frequency in frequency_window
438            ]
439
440        elif isinstance(frequency_window, int) or isinstance(frequency_window, float):
441            self.index_window = [
442                self._get_frequency_index(frequency_window) - 1,
443                self._get_frequency_index(frequency_window) + 1,
444            ]
445
446        self.peak_magnitude = np.max(
447            self.spectrum[:, self.index_window[0] : self.index_window[1]], axis=1
448        )
449
450        self.index_max = (
451            np.argmax(
452                self.spectrum[:, self.index_window[0] : self.index_window[1]], axis=1
453            )
454            + self.index_window[0]
455        )
456
457        self.peak_frequency_Hz = self.frequencies[self.index_max]
458
459        self.info["process_history"].append(
460            f"Peak magnitude and frequency calculated in the window {frequency_window}"
461        )
462
463        return self
